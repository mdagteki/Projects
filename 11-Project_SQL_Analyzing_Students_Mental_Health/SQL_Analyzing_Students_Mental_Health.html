Students Mental Health

Introduction

The transition to university life, especially in a foreign country, can be a significant life event that may impact a students mental health. In 2018, a Japanese international university conducted a comprehensive survey to investigate this phenomenon. The following year, they published a study that received approval from multiple ethical and regulatory boards.

Study Findings

The study revealed that international students face a heightened risk of mental health challenges compared to the general population. Two key factors emerged as predictors of depression: social connectedness, which refers to the sense of belonging to a social group, and acculturative stress, the stress associated with adapting to a new culture.

Data Exploration

To delve deeper into these findings, we will analyze the students dataset using PostgreSQL. Our goal is to determine whether similar conclusions can be drawn regarding the mental health of international students and to assess if the duration of their stay influences their well-being.

Data Description

Below is a description of the dataset columns that will aid in our analysis:

Field Name Description
inter_dom Types of students (international or domestic)
japanese_cate Japanese language proficiency
english_cate English language proficiency
academic Current academic level (undergraduate or graduate)
age Current age of student
stay Current length of stay in years
todep Total score of depression (PHQ-9 test)
tosc Total score of social connectedness (SCS test)
toas Total score of acculturative stress (ASISS test)

# Query 3: Analyze the distribution of students by Region, Gender, and Academic level
query3 = """WITH region_total AS (
                SELECT 
                    region,
                    COUNT(*) AS Total_by_Region
                FROM students
                WHERE region IS NOT NULL AND gender IS NOT NULL AND academic IS NOT NULL
                GROUP BY region
            ),
            global_total AS (
                SELECT 
                    COUNT(*) AS Total_Global
                FROM students
                WHERE region IS NOT NULL AND gender IS NOT NULL AND academic IS NOT NULL
            )
            SELECT 
                s.region,
                CASE
                    WHEN (s.gender IS NULL AND s.academic IS NULL) THEN 'Total for Region'
                    WHEN (s.region IS NULL AND s.gender IS NULL AND s.academic IS NULL) THEN 'Global Total'
                    ELSE s.gender
                END AS gender,
                CASE 
                    WHEN s.academic IS NULL THEN 'All Academics'
                    ELSE s.academic 
                END AS academic,
                COUNT(*) AS Total_Students,
                ROUND(COUNT(*) * 100.0 / r.Total_by_Region, 2) AS Percentage_by_Region,
                ROUND(COUNT(*) * 100.0 / g.Total_Global, 2) AS Global_Percentage
            FROM students s
            JOIN region_total r ON s.region = r.region
            CROSS JOIN global_total g
            WHERE s.region IS NOT NULL AND s.gender IS NOT NULL AND s.academic IS NOT NULL
            GROUP BY ROLLUP (s.region, s.gender, s.academic), r.Total_by_Region, g.Total_Global
            ORDER BY s.region, s.gender, s.academic
            LIMIT 10;"""

# Execute the query and store the result in a DataFrame
result3 = duckdb.query(query3).to_df()

# Query 4: Distribution of International and Domestic Students by Age and Language Proficiency
query4 = """
/* This query calculates the distribution of international and domestic students,
   along with their average age, average length of stay, and average language proficiency scores. */
SELECT 
    CASE
        WHEN inter_dom = 'Dom' THEN 'Domestic'
        WHEN inter_dom = 'Inter' THEN 'International'
    END AS origin,
    gender,
    COUNT(gender) AS no_of_students,
    ROUND(AVG(age),2) AS avg_age,
    ROUND(AVG(stay),2) AS avg_stay_year,
    ROUND(AVG(japanese),2) AS avg_japanese_lang,
    ROUND(AVG(english),2) AS avg_english_lang    
FROM students
WHERE inter_dom IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY ROLLUP (inter_dom, gender)
ORDER BY origin, gender;
"""

# Execute the query and store the result in a DataFrame
result4 = duckdb.query(query4).to_df()

# Query 6: Average Mental Health Scores by Length of Stay for International Students
query6 = """
/* This query calculates the average mental health scores for international students,
   grouped by their length of stay, and displays the results in descending order of stay. */
SELECT 
    stay,
    ROUND(AVG(todep), 2) AS average_phq, 
    ROUND(AVG(tosc), 2) AS average_scs, 
    ROUND(AVG(toas), 2) AS average_as
FROM students
WHERE inter_dom = 'Inter'
GROUP BY stay
ORDER BY stay DESC;
"""

# Execute the query and store the result in a DataFrame
result6 = duckdb.query(query6).to_df()

Visualizations of the Data

The visualizations presented in this project serve as a powerful tool for interpreting the complex data on international students mental health. The histograms offer a visual representation of the distribution of mental health scores (PHQ, SCS, AS) across different lengths of stay. A clear pattern emerges from the histograms: as the length of stay increases, certain mental health challenges become more prevalent. This suggests that the duration of exposure to a new cultural environment may correlate with the level of stress experienced by students.
