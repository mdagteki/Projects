Hypothesis Testing in Healthcare: Drug Safety¶

A pharmaceutical company GlobalXYZ has just completed a randomized controlled drug trial. To promote transparency and reproducibility of the drug's outcome, they (GlobalXYZ) have presented the dataset to your organization, a non-profit that focuses primarily on drug safety.

The dataset provided contained five adverse effects, demographic data, vital signs, etc. Your organization is primarily interested in the drug's adverse reactions. It wants to know if the adverse reactions, if any, are of significant proportions. It has asked you to explore and answer some questions from the data.

The dataset drug_safety.csv was obtained from Hbiostat courtesy of the Vanderbilt University Department of Biostatistics. It contained five adverse effects: headache, abdominal pain, dyspepsia, upper respiratory infection, chronic obstructive airway disease (COAD), demographic data, vital signs, lab measures, etc. The ratio of drug observations to placebo observations is 2 to 1.

For this project, the dataset has been modified to reflect the presence and absence of adverse effects adverse_effects and the number of adverse effects in a single individual num_effects.

The columns in the modified dataset are:

Column Description
sex The gender of the individual
age The age of the individual
week The week of the drug testing
trx The treatment (Drug) and control (Placebo) groups
wbc The count of white blood cells
rbc The count of red blood cells
adverse_effects The presence of at least a single adverse effect
num_effects The number of adverse effects experienced by a single individual

The Mann-Whitney U test is a non-parametric test used to assess whether two independent samples come from the same population or have significantly different distributions. In this case, we're interested in determining if there is a significant difference in the ages between the Drug and Placebo groups.

After confirming that the age data is not normally distributed using Shapiro-Wilk tests, which is a test for normality, we opted for the Mann-Whitney U test due to its robustness against non-normal distributions.

The Mann-Whitney U test calculates a U statistic, which represents the sum of ranks of one sample relative to the other. The test then generates a p-value, which indicates the probability of observing the obtained U statistic (or one more extreme) under the assumption that the distributions of both samples are identical.

In the code snippet provided, u_statistic represents the computed U statistic, while age_group_effects_p_value holds the resulting p-value of the test. This p-value is crucial for determining the statistical significance of the observed difference in ages between the Drug and Placebo groups.

If the p-value is less than a chosen significance level (commonly α = 0.05), it suggests that there is sufficient evidence to reject the null hypothesis, indicating a significant difference in ages between the two groups. Conversely, if the p-value is greater than α, we fail to reject the null hypothesis, implying no significant difference in ages between the groups.

Therefore, the age_group_effects_p_value obtained from the Mann-Whitney U test serves as a key metric for assessing the significance of age disparities between the Drug and Placebo groups in our analysis.

With a p-value of 0.256962 obtained from the Mann-Whitney U test, we interpret this result in the context of our hypothesis testing.

Since the p-value is greater than the commonly chosen significance level of 0.05 (α = 0.05), we fail to reject the null hypothesis. In other words, there is not enough evidence to suggest a significant difference in ages between the Drug and Placebo groups.

This suggests that, based on the age data available in our analysis, there is no statistically significant disparity in ages between individuals who received the drug treatment and those who received the placebo. Therefore, age does not seem to be a significant factor in distinguishing between the two groups in our study.

It's important to note that while there may not be a significant difference in ages between the groups, other factors could still influence the outcomes or effectiveness of the drug treatment.
